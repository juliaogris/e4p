Tell me about The idea

  • Collect unused but operational laptops from friends and families.
  • Setup a local install of the Khanacademy (Videos and exercises) under Ubuntu for performance and licence reasons.
  • Take laptops in needy community. Electricity required, but no internet access.
  • Find local supporter to coach and setup new students.
  • Measure success by number of students, hours of lectures watched and completed exercises. (Khanacademy software makes this easy).

Who is the khan academy?

With a library of over 3,900 videos covering everything from arithmetic to physics, finance, and history and hundreds of practice exercises, Khanacademy is on a mission to "provide high quality education to anyone, anywhere". Watch founder Sal Khan tell his story at an awe inspiring Ted-talk.

Independent KA Lite is a standalone web app for serving core Khan Academy content - videos and exercises - from a local server.

What can I do?

If you have any unused laptops, tablets, smart phones, mice, keyboards or usb flash drives and you live in Melbourne, Sydney (Australia), Vienna (Austria) or London (UK) please contact us and we will come and pick it up. We also sincerely appreciate some word-spreading love.

Next steps

This project is in its early stages. A first batch of machines has been deployed to a school in Masaka Uganda. There are several prospect communities and schools for deployment in Borneo, India and Nepal.
